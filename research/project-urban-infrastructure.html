AI for Urban Infrastructure Assessment & Pedestrian Safety

Impact of soft infrastructure changes on pedestrian safety: a computer vision analysis across Minneapolis using existing CCTV networks.

Overview

This project deploys deep learning models on municipal video feeds to quantify how incremental urban design shifts influence pedestrian safety. Our approach taps into installed CCTV infrastructure, enabling cities to extract rich analytics without expensive hardware upgrades.

Key Capabilities

  • Integrates with existing CCTV deployments
  • Cost-effective and rapidly scalable
  • AI-based framework for multi-modal data fusion
  • Vehicle speed profiling and trend analysis
  • Stop behavior and maneuver classification
  • Traffic volume and direction analytics

Insights Enabled

Our pipeline surfaces metrics that help planners evaluate redesign effectiveness and prioritize interventions. The system produces dashboards for speed distributions, conflict points, near-miss events, and pedestrian-vehicle interactions under varying lighting and weather conditions.

Methodology

We deploy edge-optimized detection, tracking, and motion forecasting models. These modules operate on live feeds and generate structured outputs suitable for long-term statistical analysis.

  • Camera calibration and region-of-interest definition
  • Multi-object tracking across heterogeneous cameras
  • Trajectory smoothing and speed estimation
  • Behavioral classification using transformer-based encoders

Results are aggregated into interactive dashboards and geospatial overlays for urban planners:

  • Maneuver distribution heatmaps
  • Pedestrian exposure and crossing compliance
  • Vehicle stop compliance and queuing metrics
  • Temporal trends for targeted policy evaluation
